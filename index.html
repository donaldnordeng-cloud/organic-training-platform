What this is

Training built for the new organic approval regime

The Organic Products and Production Act 2023 (OPPA) has replaced New Zealand's voluntary organic certification system with a mandatory government-administered approval regime. Every operator — from kiwifruit growers to retail stores — now has statutory obligations they must understand and meet.

This platform delivers self-paced e-learning that explains what organic approval means for your specific operation, what you must do, what records you must keep, and how to stay compliant year after year. Completion records serve as evidence of your annual CPD requirement under OPPA.

How it works

One licence. Your whole team.

One annual licence covers every staff member at your operation

You purchase one licence for your business. Every employee — from the owner to seasonal workers — trains under that single licence. You nominate one person as your administrator, who manages staff access and can pull completion records at any time.

Staff must be employees of the licensed operation to access the platform. The licence is tied to your business, not to individual people.

Your administrator can see who has completed which modules, download certificates, and manage access — without needing to contact us. Records are retained within the platform and renew annually with your licence.

On-farm contractors have two options: they can train under one of the operator licences they work for, or hold their own contractor licence covering all their staff. Contractors who work across multiple operations may find their own licence the most practical option.
